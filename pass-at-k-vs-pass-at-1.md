# Pass@k vs Pass@1: Does RL Here Sharpen the Policy or Expand It?

_Status: idea · Started: 2026-05-12 · Last updated: 2026-05-12_
_Source: distributional-lens framing in `SKILLS.md` / `meta-algorithm-alpha-lambda.md`; prompted by the "RL sharpens vs. expands" debate (Yue et al. 2025 vs. ProRL) and the need to make pass@k first-class **before** reading too much into the RL sweep's `reward` curves (`harness/run_h100_sweep.sh`)._

## Introduction
**Question.** When the harness's RL corner lifts `reward` (≈ pass@1), is it *acquiring new solution trajectories* or just *re-weighting mass* onto ones the un-RL'd init model already had — i.e., does pass@k stay flat (or **fall**) while pass@1 rises? And how does that sharpening↔expansion balance move with the (α, λ, π_T) corner (SFT / RL / OPD / OPSD), eval temperature, the k-range you test, and base- vs. SFT-init?

**The literature (the back-and-forth).** Yue et al. 2025 ("Does Reinforcement Learning Really Incentivize Reasoning Capacity in LLMs Beyond the Base Model?", arXiv:2504.13837, NeurIPS 2025 best-paper runner-up) is the canonical statement: RLVR reliably lifts pass@1, but crank k and the **base** model's pass@k catches or beats the RLVR model's — six popular RLVR algorithms behave alike — so RLVR isn't adding reasoning trajectories, it's re-weighting mass onto solutions the base could already reach, at the cost of coverage/diversity (with the matching entropy collapse); they note *distillation* is what actually introduces new patterns. ProRL (Liu et al. 2025, "Prolonged Reinforcement Learning Expands Reasoning Boundaries in Large Language Models", arXiv:2505.24864, NVIDIA → Nemotron-Research-Reasoning-Qwen-1.5B) is the counterpoint: *prolonged* RL with the right recipe — KL control, periodic **hard reset** of the reference policy + optimizer, a diverse task suite, enough compute — genuinely expands the reachable set. Follow-ups stress the conclusion is sensitive to the k-range, decoding temperature, prompt format, task family, and whether RL starts from a strong SFT init. Honest summary: **"default RLVR recipes over-sharpen the policy"**, not "RL provably can't expand capability."

**Why it matters here.** The whole (α, λ, π_T) framing is *which objective puts mass where*: verifier-reward RL concentrates mass on high-reward modes; reverse-KL distillation (OPD/OPSD) is *even more* mode-seeking than RL's surrogate (Gu et al. 2023) — so on the sharpening axis you'd predict the pass@k / diversity cost is ordered roughly **forward-KL SFT < RL < reverse-KL OPD/OPSD** (OPD/OPSD sharpest; SFT flattest *on-task*, though it pays the biggest *off-task* cost). That ordering is the empirical content of [[entropy-collapse-opd-vs-rl]] (#3); it also touches [[student-beats-teacher-opd]] (#2 — on-policy coverage vs. "KL-matching ≠ reward-maximization"), [[sparse-vs-dense-updates]] (#5 — the parameter-geometry side: "RL = small but *essential* re-weighting" is the same story from the output side), and [[expert-rl-plus-opd]] / [[prms-as-teachers]] (#11/#12 — the λ-blend as the sharpening↔coverage dial). And practically: **the harness logs only `reward`** — one greedy-ish rollout per prompt scored by reasoning_gym, i.e. a pass@1-flavored / average-accuracy metric — so without pass@k you literally can't tell a "good" RL run from one that's just collapsed onto one trick. Build pass@k + diversity as a first-class eval before over-reading the sweep that's running now (job `71114`).

**Prior work.** Yue et al. 2025 (arXiv:2504.13837); Liu et al. 2025 — ProRL (arXiv:2505.24864) + ProRL v2; Chen et al. 2021 (the unbiased pass@k estimator — Codex/HumanEval); Gu et al. 2023 (reverse-KL mode collapse / GKD); Lu & Thinking Machines 2025 (OPD); Zhao et al. 2026 (OPSD — per-token KL clip stops the collapse within ~100 steps); Schulman et al. 2025 (O(1) bits/episode for outcome RL); Brown 2026 (capability-vs-KL Pareto).

## Data
What is the data? An **eval set**, not a training set — plus the standard battery so results compose (cf. `meta-algorithm-alpha-lambda.md`).
- **Tasks (reuse what's wired into `policy_gradients` / the harness configs):** a **narrow** task — `spell_backward` (the configs' current placeholder: short, near-deterministic answer → expect over-sharpening *loudest* here) — and a **wider** task with many legitimate distinct correct solutions / longer CoT (a multi-step arithmetic or word-puzzle reasoning_gym task) — plus a **held-out general slice** for the forgetting axis.
- **Held-out prompts:** a fixed eval set of `N` prompts per task (≈ 256–512), generated once with a fixed seed, never seen in training.
- **Per eval, logged:** for `k ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256}` — the **unbiased pass@k** (Chen et al. 2021: sample `n ≥ max_k` completions/prompt, `pass@k = 1 − C(n−c, k)/C(n, k)`, averaged over prompts, `c` = #correct); **pass@1** (≈ the `reward` metric — sanity-check it tracks); a **coverage proxy on the correct set** — distinct-n (n=1..4), self-BLEU (lower = more diverse), empirical token-entropy of the solutions; evaluated at ≥ 2 decoding temperatures (≈ 0.6 = the training temp, and ≈ 1.0). **During training** (already logged or cheap to add): policy entropy, `off_policy/max_level`, KL-to-base — the *leading* indicators; pass@k on the fixed set is the *lagging* confirmation.
- **Concrete example — what the artifact looks like (illustrative numbers):**
  `(corner=base/init, task=spell_backward, T=1.0): pass@1 0.34, pass@128 0.98, distinct-4 0.62`
  `(corner=rl_grpo,   task=spell_backward, T=1.0, step=500): pass@1 0.78, pass@8 0.91, pass@128 0.96, distinct-4 0.31`
  → pass@1 way up, pass@128 *down*, solution diversity halved, crossover at some `k* < 128`.
- **Preprocessing:** correctness `c` straight from the reasoning_gym verifier (`_pg.compute_rewards`), so "correct" is defined identically to training; n-grams over the model's *generated* tokens (post-chat-template strip).

## Method and model
**High-level:** a new eval module — `harness/eval_passk.py`, the README's stubbed "eval hooks" made real — that, given (checkpoint, reasoning_gym task spec, `k_max`, `n_samples`, temperatures): samples `n` completions per held-out prompt (batched, reusing `_pg.rollout` so generation/scoring is identical to training), computes the unbiased pass@k curve + pass@1 + the diversity proxies, logs an `eval/*` metric family + a JSON artifact. Hooked into `unified_trainer.py` every `cfg.eval_every` steps (new config field) and once at the end; also runnable standalone (`python -m harness.eval_passk --ckpt … --task … --temps 0.6,1.0`).

**Module-by-module:**
- *Sampler* — `_pg.rollout(model, entries, dataset, tokenizer, max_new_tokens, temperature, top_p, top_k, min_p)` over the held-out entries, `n_samples` rollouts/entry, in `model.eval()` + `torch.no_grad()`; `_pg.compute_rewards` (the reasoning_gym verifier) → per-completion correctness. **No new generation code.**
- *pass@k estimator* — Chen et al. 2021, numerically stable form `1 − ∏_{i=0}^{k-1} (n−c−i)/(n−i)`, vectorized over prompts; report mean ± bootstrap CI over prompts; also the **crossover k** (smallest k where the trained model's pass@k ≤ the init's), if it exists.
- *Diversity proxies* — distinct-n (unique n-grams / total, over the *correct* completions per prompt, averaged); self-BLEU (mean pairwise BLEU among correct completions); token-entropy (mean per-position entropy of the next-token distribution along sampled correct completions, teacher-forced — one cheap forward pass). Reuse `_pg.compute_log_probs` machinery for the entropy estimate.
- *Logging* — W&B line plots (pass@k vs. k, one line/corner; pass@1 & pass@k overlaid vs. training step; entropy vs. step) + the `eval/<corner>_<task>_<temp>.json` artifact for offline plots; via `wandb_logging.Logger`.

**Training setup / infra.** No new *training* code for the measurement — the *experiment* is running the existing corners with eval hooks on and sweeping the axes below. The RL corner = the sweep job `71114` (re-run with eval on, or eval its final checkpoints); OPD/OPSD once #6's `lam>0` path lands; SFT once that corner lands. W&B project `distill-harness`; SLURM `bigTiger` — the H100 node does the eval passes too (`n·N·#temps` generations per (corner, task) checkpoint ≈ 256K for `n=256, N=512, 2 temps` — batched on an H100 with a 1B model, minutes).

**Axes (the actual study):**
- *corner*: base/init · SFT · RL (grpo, + the other objectives in the sweep) · OPD (real same-family teacher) · OPSD (answer-conditioned self-teacher + per-token KL clip) · a couple of (α, λ) interior points.
- *eval temperature*: ≈ 0.6 (training temp) and ≈ 1.0 (and maybe 1.2).
- *k-range*: up to 256 — report the crossover k (the Yue-et-al. sensitivity is exactly this).
- *init*: base-init vs. SFT-init (only once the SFT corner is real).
- *recipe knobs on the RL/OPD runs*: entropy-bonus coeff {0, small, larger}; OPSD-style per-token KL clip on/off; KL-to-base `β` {0, small}; `λ` for the expert-RL+OPD interior — does turning these on buy back pass@k at a small pass@1 cost (the ProRL direction)?
- *training compute*: pass@k at 100 / 250 / 500 / (if cheap) more steps — gap widens monotonically, or does prolonged-RL-with-the-right-knobs recover it (ProRL, scaled down)?

**Ablations.** `n_samples` (does pass@k stabilize by n=128?); diversity-proxy choice (do distinct-n / self-BLEU / token-entropy agree?); greedy-pass@1 vs. sampled-pass@1 as the "pass@1" line; task width (narrow `spell_backward` vs. the wider task — effect loudest on narrow); verifier-noise stress (does a slightly noisy `c` flip the crossover?).

- **Comparison to baselines:** the base/init pass@k curve *is* the baseline. Secondary: the *reported direction* in Yue et al. (trained ≤ base at large k; six RLVR algos alike) and ProRL (trained > base with the right recipe) — we expect to land between, recipe-dependent. Small model, short training, toy reasoning_gym tasks → read the **ordering** and the **recovery effect**, not absolute numbers.
- **Plots / curves (link the files):** `eval/passk_vs_k.png` (one line/corner, init highlighted); `eval/pass1_vs_passk_over_steps.png` (does pass@k fall while pass@1 rises?); `eval/entropy_vs_step.png` overlaid; the per-(corner, task, temp) `eval/*.json`.
- **Where it works / breaks:** effect strongest on the narrow task + low eval temperature + base-init + no entropy bonus; weakest (or reversed) on the wider task + higher temperature + SFT-init + entropy bonus / KL control. OPD/OPSD sharpest; SFT flattest. **Breaks if:** the toy tasks have little legitimate solution diversity (then "collapse" is harmless and uninteresting — the main risk with `spell_backward`, hence the wider task); the verifier is noisy (`c` miscounted → pass@k biased); `n` too small to estimate large-k pass@k; "matched compute" across corners is hard to define fairly.

## Takeaways *(predictions)*
- (To fill in.) Hypotheses going in: (1) default RL here lifts pass@1 and **erodes the pass@k-gap-vs-init** on the narrow task — a crossover k exists; (2) the sharpening order **forward-KL SFT < RL < reverse-KL OPD/OPSD** holds on the diversity proxies — confirming #3's central prediction from a different angle; (3) an entropy bonus / OPSD-style per-token KL clip / a moderate λ **partially recovers** pass@k at a small pass@1 cost — i.e. the (α, λ) interior really is a sharpening↔coverage dial, and ProRL's "right recipe" claim survives scaled down; (4) the whole effect is **sensitive enough** to k-range / temperature / init that any single number is misleading — which is itself the point, and the argument for making pass@k + entropy first-class *before* reading the `reward` curves from the current sweep.
- Risk: the toy `reasoning_gym` tasks may be too narrow for pass@k to mean much — pick (or add) a genuinely multi-solution task; raw entropy is a coarse proxy for "diversity that *matters*" (correct *alternative* solutions, not token noise); pass@k estimation needs many samples to be tight at k ≥ 128.
- Open: does the crossover k shrink monotonically with training steps (a clean "over-sharpening rate")? Can a diversity-preserving objective (forward-KL distillation, an entropy floor, PRM-reweighted OPSD from [[prms-as-teachers]]) keep pass@k ≈ init while still lifting pass@1 — i.e. is the Pareto frontier actually movable? Should the eval hook's pass@k *gate* training (early-stop / bump λ or the entropy bonus when the crossover k drops below a threshold)?
