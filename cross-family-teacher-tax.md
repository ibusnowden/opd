# The Cross-Family Teacher Tax: How Much of SFT Goes to Surface Form vs Competence?

_Status: idea · Started: 2026-05-11 · Last updated: 2026-05-11_
_Source: `research/ideas.md` — §2 "same-family vs. different-family teachers"._

## Introduction
**Question.** "Doing SFT" means choosing a teacher, and the teacher's relationship to the student is a big efficiency axis that gets ignored. A **same-family** teacher (tokenizer-matched, recipe-matched — the canonical prior-art example is Qwen3-32B → Qwen3-8B-Base; *our* setups use the OLMo-2 family, e.g. OLMo-2-7B → OLMo-2-1B, which shares one tokenizer across all sizes) produces outputs structurally close to what the student naturally generates, so the SFT signal is mostly about the *capability gap*, and per-token teacher logprobs are directly comparable to the student's (so soft-target / on-policy distillation is even *possible*). A **different-family** teacher brings two costs: (1) *tokenizer mismatch* — re-tokenize every completion in the student's vocab, lose information at boundaries, and per-token logprobs no longer correspond to "same prediction at same position" (soft-target distillation essentially impossible without expensive workarounds); (2) *recipe mismatch* — the teacher's outputs carry stylistic/structural artifacts (list formatting, when it uses CoT, register) that are pipeline byproducts, not capabilities, and the student burns SFT bits learning surface form. Can we **measure** the recipe-mismatch tax — what fraction of cross-family SFT bits go to surface form rather than competence — and the tokenizer-mismatch cost?

**Why it matters.** This determines *which move on the algorithm tree is even available*: on-policy distillation (the efficient one) is essentially same-family-only (you need tokenizer match just to compute the per-token KL, and approximate recipe match for that signal to be informative). So "is my teacher same-family?" gates the entire `meta-algorithm-alpha-lambda.md` / `hint-*` line of work. And in practice people *do* cross-family distill (frontier-closed → open base) — quantifying the tax tells you how much you're paying, when a recipe-normalization preprocessing step is worth it, and when you should just eat the RL sparsity tax instead.

**Prior work.** The post (§2, §3); Qwen3 technical report 2025 (same-family OPD, the canonical Qwen3-32B→8B-Base example); Lu & Thinking Machines 2025 (OPD post, revisits same-family); cross-tokenizer distillation methods (ULD / MinED-style token-alignment, "universal logit distillation"); Shenfeld et al. 2026 (SDFT — privileged-info conditioning is automatically tokenizer/recipe-matched because it's the same model); Brown 2026 (capability-vs-KL framing); Ross et al. 2010 (exposure bias — relevant to why even same-family off-policy SFT caps short of teacher quality).

## Data
- **One held-out task with a clean verifier** (math from `reasoning_gym` / AIME-style, in `policy_gradients`; optionally Minimal Code Editing as a second domain), where a *capability* gap to the teacher exists.
- **Teachers spanning the family axis** (concrete picks — student is always `allenai/OLMo-2-0425-1B`, so OLMo-2 = "same family"): (a) **same-family, larger scale** — `allenai/OLMo-2-1124-7B(-Instruct)` (tokenizer + recipe match; this is the clean baseline, and the reason we standardize on OLMo); (b) **same-tokenizer, different recipe** — a differently-post-trained OLMo-2-7B sibling (e.g. base vs Instruct vs an RLHF'd variant); (c) **different tokenizer, similar recipe** — a comparably-sized open model from another family (e.g. Llama-3.x-8B / Qwen2.5-7B); (d) **different tokenizer and recipe** — a genuinely foreign frontier-style teacher. Match teacher *capability* on the task as closely as possible across (a)–(d) so the comparison isolates the *form* tax, not the *competence* available. Start at the 1B←7B scale on a single **RTX GPU**; scale later.
- For each teacher: raw completions + a *recipe-normalized* version (re-rendered into the student's house style/format, e.g. via a cheap rewrite that preserves content) + the re-tokenization mapping.
- Logged: SFT loss decomposed by token type (content tokens vs formatting/discourse tokens — via a classifier or proxy), per-token logprob comparability stats (KL between teacher & student at matched positions, where definable), boundary-information-loss from re-tokenization (entropy of the segmentation), downstream pass@1, forgetting on a general benchmark, "how teacher-styled is the student" (a style classifier: does it look like a worse copy of the teacher's surface form?).

## Method and model
**Design — same competence, vary the family distance.** Run teacher-SFT from the *same base* with each teacher (a)–(d), at matched amounts of SFT data, holding teacher task-capability fixed. Then:
1. **Recipe-mismatch tax.** Compare (b)/(c)/(d) vs (a) on: (i) downstream task gain per SFT token (efficiency), (ii) fraction of the SFT loss/gradient mass that lands on formatting/discourse tokens vs content tokens, (iii) how "teacher-styled" the resulting student is (style classifier). The gap (a)−(d) on (i), attributable to (ii)/(iii), *is* the tax. Then add the recipe-normalization preprocessing to the foreign-teacher arms and see how much of the tax it recovers.
2. **Tokenizer-mismatch cost.** For (c)/(d): measure boundary-information loss from re-tokenization, and the fraction of teacher completions where per-token logprobs are *not* position-comparable to the student's; attempt a cross-tokenizer soft-target method (token-alignment) and measure what it buys vs hard-target SFT.
3. **Availability of OPD.** Confirm directly that on-policy distillation is feasible for (a)/(b) (shared tokenizer) and breaks / needs expensive workarounds for (c)/(d); for (a) vs (b), check whether *recipe* mismatch (same tokenizer) already degrades the per-token KL signal (gradient dominated by "teacher would phrase this differently" rather than "reason differently").

**Modules.** SFT loop (shared with `sft-rl-tipping-point.md`, to be built); a token-type classifier (content vs formatting/discourse); a recipe-normalization rewriter (cheap, content-preserving — could reuse a small model); a cross-tokenizer alignment utility for the soft-target experiment; the OPD trainer from `meta-algorithm-alpha-lambda.md` for the availability check. Reuse `policy_gradients/loss.py::approx_kl`/`masked_mean`, `train.py::compute_log_probs`. W&B logging + SLURM driver à la `scripts/run_all_policy_gradients.sh` (`bigTiger`). Tiny-scale plumbing sanity on `gpt_from_scratch/run.py`.

**Ablations.** SFT-data amount; teacher capability level (does the tax shrink in relative terms when there's a bigger competence gap to amortize it against?); domain (math vs code); with/without recipe-normalization; hard-target vs cross-tokenizer soft-target for foreign teachers; whether the student is base vs lightly-instruction-tuned before SFT.

## Evaluation *(proposed — no results yet)*
| Teacher (matched capability) | tokenizer | recipe | task gain per SFT token | SFT mass on form vs content tokens | "teacher-styled" student? | OPD available? | forgetting Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| (a) same-family, larger | match | match | **highest (expected)** | low form-share | mild | **yes** | small |
| (b) same-tokenizer, diff recipe | match | mismatch | ↓ vs (a) | higher form-share (expected) | moderate | yes, but KL signal noisier (expected) | small–mod |
| (c) diff tokenizer, similar recipe | mismatch | ~match | ↓ (boundary loss) | mid | mild | **no / expensive workaround** | mod |
| (d) diff tokenizer + recipe (foreign) | mismatch | mismatch | **lowest (expected)** | **highest form-share (the tax)** | strong (looks like worse copy) | **no** | larger |
| (d) + recipe-normalization | mismatch | normalized | ↑ toward (c) (expected, recovers part of the tax) | lower | mild | still no | mod |

- **Headline metrics:** the (a)−(d) gap in task-gain-per-SFT-token, and how much of it the form-vs-content decomposition + style classifier explain (= the recipe tax); how much recipe-normalization recovers; the boundary-information loss and logprob-noncomparability rate (= the tokenizer cost); a direct demonstration that OPD is same-family-gated.
- **Expected:** a substantial recipe tax for genuinely foreign teachers (a meaningful fraction of SFT bits going to surface form), partly recoverable by normalization; tokenizer mismatch additionally costs information at boundaries and *blocks* on-policy distillation; even same-tokenizer recipe mismatch noticeably degrades the per-token KL signal — confirming "same-family SFT is much more efficient per unit of capability, even if you can't always afford to limit yourself to it."
- **Where it breaks:** "matched teacher capability" across families is hard to achieve cleanly (and is itself confounded by family); the content-vs-form token classifier is fuzzy; recipe-normalization could itself drop content (need a fidelity check); cross-tokenizer soft-target methods have many variants and results may be method-specific.

## Takeaways *(predictions)*
- Likely conclusion: the family axis is a first-order efficiency knob — cross-family SFT pays a real, partly-measurable tax in bits-spent-on-surface-form, tokenizer mismatch adds a separate boundary/comparability cost, and together they explain why the efficient move (on-policy distillation) is same-family-only. Practical upshot: prefer same-family teachers when you can; when you can't, recipe-normalize the data first and don't expect to do OPD.
- Risk: confounds between "family" and "capability"; fuzzy token classification; normalization fidelity.
- Open: how good can a *learned* recipe-normalizer get — enough to make a foreign frontier teacher behave like a same-family one for OPD purposes (re-tokenize + re-style + align logprobs)? That would reopen the whole `hint-*` / OPD line for cross-family teachers.
